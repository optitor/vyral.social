### Critical Problems

<div className="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-6">
  Content creators receive a fraction of the advertising revenue they generate,
  often as little as 30-40%, with the platform retaining the majority.
  Meanwhile, regular users who drive engagement through likes, comments, and
  shares receive nothing despite being essential to the platform's value
  creation.
</div>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Unsustainable Creator Income

    Creators lack sustainable income streams, forcing reliance on volatile brand deals and algorithm changes that can devastate earnings overnight.

  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### No User Control

    Users have no control over platform governance, content moderation, or feature development despite being primary stakeholders.

  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Data Exploitation

    Centralized platforms collect vast amounts of user data without meaningful compensation or transparency about its use.

  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Platform Lock-In

    Lack of interoperability forces users to fragment attention across multiple apps, each with closed ecosystems.

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout title="‚ö†Ô∏è Previous Attempts Failed" type="warn">
  While blockchain demonstrated alternative models are possible, most
  decentralized social media attempts failed due to **poor UX**, **complex
  onboarding**, and **unsustainable tokenomics** that prioritize speculation
  over utility.
</Callout>
